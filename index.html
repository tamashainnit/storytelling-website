  1. story 1 北海道の農家、佐藤さんは、一年を通じて自然と共に生きています。春になると、雪が溶けて畑を耕す時期が来ます。暖かくなれば、早く種をまくことができます。夏には、美しい花や作物が育ちます。雨が少なければ、水をやらなければなりません。秋になると、作物の収穫が始まります。作物が育っていたら、家族全員で手伝います。そして、冬には重たい雪が降ります。雪がたくさん降ったら、道を除雪しなければなりません。このように、季節が変わるたびに、佐藤さんは新しい挑戦に直面します。しかし、彼は自然のリズムに従って生活することを好みます。天気が良ければ、彼は満足感を得ます。北海道の美しい四季が、佐藤さんの暮らしを豊かにしているのです。 Phrase by phrase 北海道ほっかいどう のの 農家のうか、佐藤さとう さんさん はは、一年いちねん をを 通じてつうじて 自然しぜん とと 共にともに 生きていますいきています。 Mr. Sato, a farmer from Hokkaido, lives in harmony with nature throughout the year. The phrase 〜と共に (〜とともに) means 'together with' or 'in conjunction with.' The verb 生きる (いきる) means 'to live' and is used in its ~て form (生きています), indicating a continuous or habitual action, 'is living.' The structure 一年を通じて (いちねんをつうじて) translates to 'throughout the year.' 春はるにになるなるとと、雪ゆきがが溶けてとけて畑はたけをを耕すたがやす時期じきがが来ますきます。 When spring comes, the snow melts and the time to till the fields arrives. The phrase uses 'なると' to indicate a temporal or conditional relationship, meaning 'when' or 'upon'. The use of the 'て' form in '溶けて' connects verbs, expressing actions in sequence. '来ます' is the verb form that indicates something is coming or arriving, in this case, a particular time period. 暖かくあたたかく なればなれば、早くはやく 種たねをまくまくことができますできます。 When it becomes warm, you can sow seeds early. The phrase uses the conditional form 'なれば,' which means 'if' or 'when.' It indicates a condition that, when met, leads to the result described in the main clause. The structure 'ことができます' indicates the ability to do something, translating to 'can' or 'able to do' in English. 夏なつ にに はは 、 美しいうつくしい 花はな やや 作物さくもつ がが 育ちますそだちます 。 In summer, beautiful flowers and crops grow. The particle 'には' is used here to specify the time 'in summer' with emphasis. '育ちます' is the polite form of the verb '育つ' (to grow), indicating that the sentence is in the non-past, formal form, describing a habitual action. 雨あめが少なければすくなければ、水みずをやらなければなりませんやらなければなりません。 If there is little rain, we must water. The phrase uses the conditional form '-ば' (in this case, '少なければ') to express a condition 'if there is little rain'. It also uses 'なければならない' which means 'must' or 'have to'. This implies necessity, and in this context, it's paired with watering, suggesting that it becomes necessary to water in lack of rain. 秋あき にに なるとなると、 作物さくもつ のの 収穫しゅうかく がが 始まりますはじまります。 When autumn comes, the harvest of crops begins. The phrase uses the conditional form 'になると' (when it becomes), which indicates a change or a condition leading to an event. '始まります' (begins) is the polite form of the verb '始まる' (to begin/start). This construction is used to describe a sequence of events or changes related to seasons. 作物さくもつ がが 育ってそだって いたらいたら、家族かぞく 全員ぜんいん でで 手伝いますてつだいます。 If the crops had grown, the whole family helps. The sentence uses the conditional form 'たら', which indicates a condition or 'if' statement. In this structure, it expresses a condition being met (crops have grown) and the resulting action (the family helps). The verb '育っていたら' is the past progressive form of '育つ' (to grow), indicating a hypothetical state where the crops have already reached a grown state. '家族全員' means 'the whole family', used here as the subject of '手伝います' (to help), indicating who will assist once the crops have grown. そしてそして、冬ふゆにはは重たいおもたい雪ゆきが降りますふります。 And, in winter, heavy snow falls. The phrase uses the particle 'には' to specify the time context, '冬' (fuyu, winter), highlighting the general time when the action occurs. The verb '降ります' (furimasu) means 'falls' and is in the polite present form. The adjective '重たい' (omotai) means 'heavy' and is describing the snow '雪' (yuki). 'そして' is a conjunction meaning 'and' or 'then', used to connect this sentence with the previous one. 雪ゆきがたくさん降ふったら、道みちを除雪じょせつしなければなりません。 If a lot of snow falls, we must clear the roads. This sentence uses the conditional form 'たら' to indicate a condition ('if a lot of snow falls'). The verb '降る' (furu) meaning 'to fall' is in its past tense '降った' to form '降ったら' ('if it falls'). 'しなければなりません' is a formal way to express obligation, meaning 'must do.' The phrase '除雪する' means 'to clear snow', combining '除' (remove) and '雪' (snow). このようにこのように、季節きせつが変かわるたびに、佐藤さとうさんは新あたらしい挑戦ちょうせんに直面ちょくめんします。 In this way, every time the season changes, Mr. Sato faces a new challenge. The phrase uses 'たびに' which means 'every time' or 'whenever'. It is used to express a repeated situation or occurrence. '直面します' means 'to face' or 'to confront,' showing that Mr. Sato encounters challenges regularly. しかししかし、彼かれは自然しぜんのリズムりずむに従ってしたがって生活せいかつすることを好みますこのみます。 However, he prefers to live according to the rhythm of nature. The phrase uses the verb '従って' (じゅうがって), which means 'to follow' or 'to obey.' It is used in its te-form here to connect the clauses smoothly. The structure '生活することを好みます' indicates a preference, using 'こと' to nominalize the verb '生活する' (to live), turning it into an activity that can be liked or preferred. The word 'しかし' is a conjunction meaning 'however,' which indicates a contrast with the previous statement. 天気てんきがが良ければよければ、、彼かれはは満足感まんぞくかんをを得ますえます。。 If the weather is good, he feels a sense of satisfaction. This sentence uses the conditional form 'れば' (reba), which is often used to express a condition or hypothetical situation, similar to 'if' in English. '満足感を得ます' (manzokukan o emasu) means 'gains a sense of satisfaction,' where '満足感' (manzokukan) is a noun meaning 'satisfaction,' and '得ます' (emasu) is the verb 'to gain' or 'to obtain.' 北海道ほっかいどう のの 美しいうつくしい 四季しき がが、 佐藤さとう さんさん のの 暮らしくらし をを 豊かにゆたかに しているしている のですのです。 The beautiful four seasons of Hokkaido are enriching Mr. Sato's life. The phrase uses 'の' to indicate possession or a relationship, meaning 'of' here, as in '北海道の美しい四季' (Hokkaido's beautiful four seasons). The verb '豊かにしている' comes from '豊かにする', meaning 'to make/enrich'. The sentence ends with 'のです', providing emphasis and conveying a sense of explanation or confirmation.
